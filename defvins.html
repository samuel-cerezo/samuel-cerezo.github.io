DefVINS: Robust Visual-Inertial Odometry for Deformable Scenes

Samuel Cerezo1, Javier Civera1
1Universidad de Zaragoza
Under develop, 2025

Abstract

Deformable scenes violate the rigidity assumptions underpinning classical visual-inertial odometry (VIO), often leading to early over-fitting of non-rigid models or catastrophic drift when deformation dominates parallax. We introduce DefVINS, an observability-gated deformable visual-inertial odometry framework that separates a rigid, IMU-anchored state from a non-rigid warp represented by an embedded defor- mation graph. DefVINS is seeded by a VIO initialization that fixes scale, gravity, velocity, and IMU biases, and then activates deformation degrees of freedom progressively only when the problem is well conditioned. The gating rule monitors the spectrum of the deformation block of the Hessian and enables new nodes once conditioning thresholds are met, preventing ill-posed updates under poor excitation. Ablations confirm the benefits of observability-aware activation and IMU anchoring.
